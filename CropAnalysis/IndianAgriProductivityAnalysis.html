Indian Agri Productivity Analysis

Area vs Year Observation

The cultivated area has notably expanded, possibly facilitated by increased fertilizer use and irrigation, or through conversion of fallow land or forested areas into agricultural fields.

Crop Cultivation over the area

Rice occupies the largest cultivated area, followed by wheat, indicating their prominence in agricultural practices.

Fertilizer used in Crops

The highest fertilizer usage is observed in rice crops, followed by wheat, with other crops ranking lower in consumption.

Annual Rainfall in various States

Chhattisgarh experiences the highest annual rainfall, yet its yield is not the highest. West Bengal boasts the maximum yield. In Uttar Pradesh, Haryana, and Maharashtra, despite the heavy fertilizer usage, the yield remains modest, possibly due to lower annual rainfall.

Season suitable for yeild

The Kharif season witnesses the highest cultivation area, followed by the Rabi season.

Autumn and summer crops are cultivated over smaller areas. India achieves its maximum

yield during the Kharif season.

Fertilizer used in various states

Uttar Pradesh leads in fertilizer usage, followed by Madhya Pradesh in India.

Yearly Fertilizer uses

Fertilizer usage in fields is on the rise, indicating a growing trend in agricultural practices.

Yearly pestiside uses

The usage of pesticides in fields is increasing, reflecting a rising trend in agricultural practices.

Yearly yield of crops

Yield has steadily risen until 2014 but is now declining, potentially due to factors like climate change or declining soil fertility.
